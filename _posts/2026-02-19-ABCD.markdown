Language models evaluated on standard multiple-choice benchmarks often rely on superficial artifacts like label formatting or answer position rather than actual reasoning. To address this, our proposed bias-reduced protocol replaces structured labels with uniform ones and uses sentence similarity for full-text answer evaluation, significantly improving robustness and reducing accuracy variance by 3x with minimal performance loss.
