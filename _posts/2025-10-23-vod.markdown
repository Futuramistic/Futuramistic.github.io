As standard 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) struggles to separate objects from view-dependent lighting effects like reflections and specular highlights, we propose a data-driven enhancement that uses an additional symmetric matrix to make each Gaussian's opacity view-dependent. By suppressing specific Gaussians based on the viewing angle, our approach accurately renders complex lighting and achieves improved performance across multiple benchmark datasets while maintaining rendering speeds of approximately 100 FPS.
